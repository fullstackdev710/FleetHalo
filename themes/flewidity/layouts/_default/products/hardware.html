Frequently asked questions

Telematics involves tracking and analyzing the performance of vehicles and assets through telematic control units (TCUs) embedded within them. These units provide comprehensive monitoring of various aspects such as the vehicle's status, location, driver behavior, engine diagnostics, and precise GPS positioning. By collecting and scrutinizing real-time data from the multitude of vehicles in a fleet, telematics facilitates efficient fleet management and operational oversight.

A Telematic Control Unit (TCU) is essentially a compact onboard computer in your vehicle, capable of tracking an extensive range of details including speed, idling, fuel consumption, low tire pressure, and more. Leveraging this data can lead to significant maintenance cost reductions through enhanced vehicle monitoring or improved fuel efficiency by analyzing driving behaviors.

Telematics devices, along with other connected hardware or sensors, enable the processing and analysis of a wealth of data, such as:

  • GPS positioning
  • Vehicle speed
  • Travel distance and time
  • Idling duration
  • Instances of harsh braking and acceleration
  • Seat belt usage
  • Fuel usage
  • Vehicle malfunction indicators
  • Battery voltage and comprehensive engine information
